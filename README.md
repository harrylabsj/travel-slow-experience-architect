# Slow Travel Experience Architect
Designs meaningful slow travel experiences focused on depth over checklist tourism — living like a local, not rushing through landmarks.
## What it does
- Designs **pace and rhythm plans** with pod-based itineraries, no-transfer-day buffers, and spaciousness principles
- Creates **local integration strategies** including market shopping, café-as-office routines, and language-exchange meetups
- Builds **meaningful connection frameworks** through deep-dive themes (craft, cuisine, music) and resident-for-a-week immersion
## Example scenarios
**1. 慢游大理一个月**
> 👤 不想走马观花去旅游，想去大理待一个月，过一下'有风的地方'那种慢生活，怎么安排好？
> 🤖 输出大理慢生活月计划——第一周古城民宿（睡到自然醒、逛菜市场、人民路听民谣）、第二周喜洲古镇（学扎染、逛稻田、喝咖啡）、第三周双廊（骑车环海、看苍山）、第四周沙溪古镇整理感悟。预算：民宿月租2000-4000元（比日租省60%），推荐关注大理集市日历。

**2. 30-day slow travel through Portugal**
> 👤 Design a 30-day slow travel experience through Portugal by train. Not just sightseeing — I want to feel like I lived there.
> 🤖 Pod-based itinerary — 3 towns, 10 days each — with neighborhood-living setup (local market shopping, café-as-office, language-exchange meetups), deep-dive themes (azulejo art, port wine, fado music), and 'resident-for-a-week' immersion activities.

**3. Convert checklist itinerary to slow travel**
> 👤 Convert my usual checklist-style itinerary into a slow-travel version for Tuscany.
> 🤖 Before/after transformation — from '7 towns in 5 days' to '2 towns in 7 days' with fewer destinations, deeper experiences (olive harvest participation, nonna cooking mentorship, village festival attendance), and spaciousness principles applied throughout.
